"How much for a new furnace?" is the most common question we get on the phone. The honest answer is "it depends" — but that's not useful, so this post walks through what those dependencies actually are with real Calgary-in-2026 numbers.

We're [a local Calgary installer](/furnaces/), not a marketplace, so the numbers below are what we actually quote. Other reputable installers will land within ±10%. Anyone way under those numbers is cutting a corner you can't see; anyone way over is marking up the brand.

## The headline numbers

For a standard 2,000 sq ft Calgary home replacing a single-stage gas furnace with a new one of similar capacity:

| Tier | AFUE | Typical install cost (all-in) |
|---|---|---|
| Entry (single-stage 90-92%) | 92% | **$5,500 - $7,000** |
| Mid (two-stage 95-96%) | 96% | **$7,000 - $9,000** |
| Premium (modulating 97-98% w/ variable-speed blower) | 98% | **$9,000 - $12,000** |

"All-in" means the furnace itself, removal of the old unit, basic gas + electrical + venting work, the City of Calgary permit and gas-fitting inspection, and labour. It does not include duct modifications, electrical panel upgrades, or chimney lining work — those are extra and we'll flag them at the quote.

## What you're paying for at each tier

### Entry (92% AFUE, single-stage)

The cheapest unit that meets current Alberta code (90%+ AFUE on new installs). One heat output level — full blast or off. Reliable, simple, and what we install when the home is small, the budget is tight, or the homeowner is planning to move within 5 years.

Brands at this tier we trust: AirEase, Lennox Merit series, Carrier Comfort series. Avoid: anything called a "builder grade" off-brand from a big-box store — the warranties and part availability are a nightmare in Calgary.

### Mid (96% AFUE, two-stage)

Two heat output levels — about 65% on low, 100% on high. Most calls in cold weather run on low, which means quieter operation, more even temperature, and modestly better efficiency. Sweet spot for most Calgary homes.

Brands at this tier we recommend: [AirEase A96 series](/air-ease-furnaces-calgary/), Carrier Performance series, Lennox Elite. The AirEase pricing is competitive because we're a Pro Team dealer and pass the dealer pricing through.

### Premium (97-98% AFUE, modulating + variable-speed blower)

Variable output (typically 40-100% in fine increments) paired with an ECM variable-speed blower. The result is dramatically more even temperatures, quieter operation, and the lowest gas bill of the three tiers. Justified for larger homes, homes with hot/cold spots, or homeowners staying long-term.

Brands at this tier: AirEase A97 modulating, Carrier Infinity, Lennox SLP98V.

## What pushes the price up

The numbers above are for a straightforward swap. The most common things that add to a quote:

**Chimney liner ($800 - $1,500).** If you're going from a mid-efficiency furnace to a high-efficiency condensing one and the house still uses a chimney for the water-heater vent, the chimney often needs a stainless liner because the water-heater exhaust is no longer mixed with hot furnace exhaust to keep it warm.

**Ductwork modifications ($500 - $3,000).** Older homes sometimes have undersized returns or supply ducts that hurt furnace performance. A new high-efficiency furnace with a variable-speed blower can expose duct issues that a 25-year-old fixed-speed blower masked.

**Gas line upgrade ($300 - $800).** A premium modulating furnace with a high-input first stage may need 3/4" gas line where a 1/2" was fine for an older single-stage. Required for safe BTU delivery.

**Electrical panel work ($400 - $1,200).** Variable-speed ECM blowers often need a dedicated circuit. If your panel is full or doesn't have a 15A space, electrical work is needed.

**Removal complications ($200 - $500).** Tight basement access, asbestos-wrapped ducts on pre-1980s installs, or a furnace bolted to a concrete pad that needs demolition.

We flag every one of these in writing before the install — no surprise charges on the day. If a quote doesn't mention any of these, ask why.

## What pulls the price down

**Cash vs financing.** [Financeit](/financing/) financing is interest-bearing — convenient, but you'll pay more over time. Cash buyers occasionally get a 2-3% courtesy discount.

**Off-season installs.** December through February is peak demand and prices stay firm. May through August is quieter and we occasionally have promotional pricing on AirEase units.

**Replacing AC at the same time.** If you bundle a [furnace + AC](/air-conditioning/) replacement, the labour overlap saves $400-800 versus doing them separately.

**Existing condensate drain and venting.** If your old furnace was already high-efficiency (90%+), the condensate drain and PVC venting are likely re-usable — saving 1-2 hours of plumbing work.

## Hidden costs to watch for

Things that should NOT be on a legitimate Calgary furnace quote:

**"Permit fee" line item over $250.** The Calgary residential gas permit is in the $150-200 range. Anything over that is markup.

**"Disposal fee."** Removal of the old unit is standard and should be included.

**"Surge protector" or "smart thermostat" upsells presented as required.** They're optional. A basic programmable thermostat works fine on every modern furnace.

**"Diagnostic fee" credited toward install.** This is a sales tactic that often inflates the install price to cover the "credit." We don't do it.

## When repair beats replacement

Not every furnace needs replacing. If your furnace is under 15 years old and the failure is:

- **Igniter or flame sensor:** $250-450 repair, get another 3-7 years
- **Inducer motor:** $400-700 repair
- **Control board:** $500-900 repair
- **Capacitor or relay:** $200-400 repair

Replacement is justified when:

- Furnace is 18+ years old (AFUE under 90%, efficiency gains alone pay back in 8-10 years)
- Heat exchanger crack (safety issue — never repair)
- Repeat failures within 24 months
- Repair cost exceeds 40% of replacement cost

We give honest repair-vs-replace assessments. If your 12-year-old furnace just needs an igniter, we'll [tell you that](/furnaces/) and do the repair.
